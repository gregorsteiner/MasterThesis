### Heat wave definitions

NWS excessive heat watch and warning system: 
daytime Hi greater than or equal to 40.6C (105F), with nighttime lows greater
than or equal to 26.7C (80F), for two consecutive days.
(Threshholds can vary based on geography)

EPA Climate Change Indicators:
A heat wave is a period of two or more consecutive days when the daily minimum apparent temperature
(the actual temperature adjusted for humidity) in a particular city exceeds the 85th percentile
